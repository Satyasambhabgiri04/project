What is Elder Abuse?

Elder abuse is any action that causes harm or distress to an older adult. It includes physical, emotional, financial, or neglectful abuse.

Signs of Elder Abuse

Physical Abuse

Unexplained bruises, burns, or injuries, or a history of frequent hospital visits.

Emotional Abuse

Depression, anxiety, or withdrawal from social activities. Verbal threats or intimidation.

Financial Exploitation

Unexplained financial transactions, missing funds, or forced changes to legal documents.

Neglect

Failure to provide basic needs such as food, shelter, or medical care.

How to Report Elder Abuse

If you suspect elder abuse, it is essential to take action. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Contact local authorities or Adult Protective Services (APS).
  • Reach out to a local elder advocacy organization.
  • Ensure the safety of the elder person involved before taking further steps.
